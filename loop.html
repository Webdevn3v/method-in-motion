Patterns Everywhere

Before you write a single line of code, you need to see what a loop actually is. It's already all around you.

📖
Read · 2 min

"I'm Loop. I notice things. I notice that your heartbeat repeats. That music has a beat. That the sun rises every single day. That's a loop."

A loop is just something that repeats. In code, instead of writing the same instruction 100 times, you write it once and tell the computer how many times to repeat it. That's it.

Think about brushing your teeth. You do the same motion — back and forth — over and over. You don't think "move brush left, move brush right, move brush left..." you just brush until you're done. A loop works the same way.

Loops make code powerful. With one loop, you can draw 1,000 circles, send 500 messages, or create an animation that plays forever.
